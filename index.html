Welcome to Washington DC

There is so much to see in Washington DC; like the White House, Lincoln Memorial, and the Washington Monument. All of these places add great value to our beautiful country. They also provide a historical education on the US and our journey.

The Martin Luther King Jr Memorial

This is The Martin Luther King Jr Memorial 1)This memorial represents one of the most iconic and influential men in the world 2) this man fought for our rights and pushed for equality no matter the cost 3) the memorial covers four acres and includes the Stone of Hope, a granite statue of Civil Rights Movement leader Martin Luther King Jr. 4)The memorial Opened 48 years after Dr. King's I Have a Dream speech on the steps of the Lincoln Memorial 5) the MLK Memorial stands 30 feet high.

The White House

This is the White House a.k.a the President's Palace 1) this is where all of our great presidents lived; some not so great 2) this is also where the president works 3) There are 412 doors, 147 windows, 28 fireplaces, 7 staircases, 132 rooms, 35 bathrooms, 6 levels but only 3 elevators.

The Lincoln Memorial

This is the Lincoln memorial 1)The Lincoln Memorial is a US National Memorial, built to honor the 16th President of the United States, Abraham Lincoln. 2) It is across from the Washington Monument. 3)There are 36 columns representing the 36 states in the country at the end of the Civil War. 4)There are also the names of 48 states above those columns. 5)These were the states in the country at the time of dedication. 6) Lincoln's Gettysburg Address is engraved on the north wall. 7) The building's south side contains photos and paintings of Abraham Lincoln.
